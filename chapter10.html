Chapter 10 - Radical Functions & Geometry

10.1 - Square Root Functions

Notes

  • Parent Function: f(x)=√x
  • Type of graph: Curve
  • Domain: {x| x ≥ 0} or [0, ∞]
  • Range: {y| y ≥ 0} or [0, ∞]
  • [0, ∞] are known as interval notations
  • How to Graph a Square Root Function?
    - Make a Table
    - Plot Points
    - Connect them to form a curve

10.2 - Simplifying Radical Functions

Notes

  • When you have an even exponent within the radical that fully simplifies to an odd exponent you must include absolute value symbols.
  • For example sqrt of x^6 will equal |x^3|.
  • Binomials of the form (a sqrt b + c sqrt d) and (a sqrt b - c sqrt d) are conjugates.
