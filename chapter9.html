9.1 - Graphing Parabolas

Notes

  • Parabolas have a line of symmetry that goes through it's maximum and minimum point(vertex).
  • If the first constant is negative, the parabola has a maximum point. If it's positive, it has a minimum point.
  • Their "x-intercepts" are their roots/solutions. There are two since the parabola intercepts the x-axis two times
  • Axis of Symmetry Formula: x=-b/2a(plug the numbers in using standard form: ax¬≤+bx+c=0)
  • MUST EQUAL 0!
  • To find the Vertex, you have to find the axis of symmetry and re-plug it into the original equation.
  • Parabolas can be graphed with f (x) = a(x - h)¬≤ + k, where (h, k) is the vertex of the parabola

9.2 - Solutions of Quadratic Equations

Notes

  • Your solutions are your x-intercepts.
  • Zeros, solutions, roots all mean the same thing, the solution of the parabola.
  • You can have 0, 1, or 2 solutions. Sometimes it can be your vertex or the points on your parabola.
  • First you have to graph the parabola(Find the vertex first!) and estimate your solution(s). Plug them/it back into the original equation to check, it should equal to 0.
  • If the integral roots cannot be found, just estimate the roots to the nearest tenth.
  • Vertex form: y= a(x-h)^2+k

9.3 - Transforming Parabolas

Notes

  • Vertical Transformation: y = x^2+3
  • Horizontal Translation: y=(x-2) ^2
  • Vertical and Horizontal Translation: y=(x+3)^2 +4
  • Reflect over x-ais: y= -x^2
  • Reflect over y-axis: y= (-x)^2
  • Stretched vertically: a>1
  • Compressed vertically: 0 less than a less than 1

9.4 - Completing the Squares

Notes

  • When you find the square root of a positive number you must find both the negative and positive square root. This could be shown by the plus or minus symbol.
  • STEPS FOR SOLVING BY COMPLETING BY SQUARES:
    - Isolate x^2 + bx
    - Complete the square (find the c-value which makes the expression a square)
    - The c-value should also be added to the other side of the equation to keep both sides equal.
    - And then solve the square root equation.

9.5 - Quadratic Formula

Notes

  • Quadratic Formula:
    ~ x = -b plus or minus sqrt of b^2 - 4ac all over 2a
    ~ You should generally use the quadratic formula when you have a word problem or if the question has decimals.
  • Discriminants:
    If b^2 - 4ac > 0: 2 real solutions
    If b^2 - 4ac = 0: 1 real solution
    If b^2 - 4ac less than 0: no real solutions
