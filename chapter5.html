5.4 - Compound Inequalities

Notes

  • An oar is an “or” statement. To be fancy “DISJUNCTION.”
  • A log is an “and” statement. To be fancy “CONJUNCTION.”
  • LESS TH "AND" - Most and problems (< or ≤)
  • GREAT"OR" - Most or problems (> or ≥)
  • Only "AND" statements can have no solution.
  • Only "OR" statements can have all real numbers.
  • Intersection: where the graphs of two inequalities overlap (transpired from AND)
  • Union: the graph produced from an inequality where at least one of the inequalities is true (transpired from OR)

5.5 - Absolute Value Inequalities

Notes

  • Make 2 cases
  • If you're trying to get a positive number, the equation inside should be the opposite, in this case - minus, and vice versa. Eg. If you are trying to get a positive 2, your absolute value should be something like: | x - 2 |, not | x + 2 |.
  • You can only have no solution for "and" problems.
  • You can only have all real numbers for "or" problems.
