Chapter 4 - Equations of Linear Functions

Challenge Problem by Ishwari :D (Answer at the bottom)

Write this equation in point-slope form.

2x+3y=6

4.1 - Graphing Equations In Slope Intercept Form

Notes

  • Slope Intercept Form ~ y = mx+b
  • x, y represent points on the line. "m" is slope. "b" is y-intercept.

4.2 - Creating Equations in Slope Intercept Form

Notes

  • Formula To Find The Slope m = (y2- y1)/ x2- x1
  • Given Slope and y-intercept ---> 1.Write Slope equation 2.Substitute "m" and "b"
  • Given Slope and a point ---> 1.Substitute "m" and the given points to solve for the y-intercept 2.Write in the equation in Slope-Intercept Form
  • Given 2 points ---> 1.Find the slope using the points 2.Substitute "m" and a point to solve for the y-intercept 3.Write the equation in Slope-Intercept Form

4.3 - Point Slope Form

Notes

  • Formula For Point Slope: y - y1= m(x - x1)
  • x1, y1 are the points on the line
  • You use point-slope form when given the slope and 1 point. In order to make it easy to graph, turn it into a slope intercept form and then graph.

4.4 - Parallel & Perpendicular Lines

Notes

  • Parallel Line: Same slope, different y-intercept (Can be written as ||)
  • Perpendicular Line: Slope as Negative Reciprocals (Can be written as upside down T)
  • Steps:
    1.Write the slope
    2. Substitute a point
    3. Find y-intercept
    4. Write in the equation asked for

Answer to the Challenge:

2x+3y = 6

3y = -2x+6

y = -2/3x+2

Since 2 is the y-intercept we know that the points will be (0, 2).

Thus y-2 = -2/3(x-0)
