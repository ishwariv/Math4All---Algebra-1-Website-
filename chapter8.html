Chapter 8 - Quadratic Expressions & Equations

8.1 - Adding and Subtracting polynomials

Notes

  • Monomial
    Number, variable or product of numbers and variables
    Ex: (18, 5x, 4xy, 6y^4)
  • Polynomial:
    - Monomial or the sum or difference of monomials
    - Ex: (5x + 48, 5x^2 + 32x + 48)
  • Binomial:
    - Polynomial with 2 terms
    - Ex: (5x + 48)
  • Trinomial
    - Polynomial with 3 terms
    - Ex: (5x^2 - 6x +10)
  • Degrees & Terms
    - 0 - constant
    - 1 - linear
    - 2 - quadratic
    - 3 - cubic
    - 4 - quartic
    - 5 - quintic
  • When there is no exponent in a monomial that is made of all constants the degree will always be constant.
  • However, 5x = linear, because x is raised to the power of 1.

8.4 - Special Products

Notes

  • (a-b) ^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2
  • (a+b) ^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2
  • (a-b) (a+b) = a^2 - b^2

8.5 - Using Distributive Property

Notes

  • Factor with GCF only when you have a binomial or a trinomial to factor
  • Always use GCF first if possible and then use factor by grouping.
  • Factor by Grouping only when you have 4 terms.
  • If the leading coefficient is negative, then the factor has to be negative. REMEMBER THE STANDARD FORM!!! Take the negative coefficient out if one or both of the a-values are negative.
  • If there is an answer like (x+5) (x+5), you need to square it to simplify.

8.6 - Factoring with a=1

Notes

  • Use the c positive method only when you have to factor a trinomial.
  • C-positive method:
    - Find the "c" value
    - Find the factors of "c" value.
    - Find the factors that have the sum equal to the "b" value.
  • When the "c" value is positive you will have only 2 positive factors or 2 negative factors.
  • When the "c" value is negative you will have only 1 negative factor and one positive factor. - +

8.8 - Difference of 2 squares

Notes

  • Difference of two squares only works when it is a binomial with 1 negative term and 1 positive term and with the same variables.
  • Like always, look for the GCF first.
  • When factoring, if your term is a squared variable and your other term is a perfect square, and one term is negative, then factor further.

8.9 - Perfect Square Trinomials

Notes

  • Factoring trinomials into perfect squares is the opposite of special products.
  • So, remember a^2+2ab+b^2=(a+b)(a+b)=(a+b)^2 and a^2-2ab-b^2=(a-b)(a-b)=(a-b)^2
  • In order for the trinomial to be a perfect square, a and b have to be perfect squares, and the middle term should be twice as much as a multiplied by b.
